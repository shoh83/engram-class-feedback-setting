3. Organization and Structure
- A: Sentences are well formed and ordered clearly within the short response or within a paragraph-length answer. The response reads smoothly at the sentence or within-paragraph level.
- B: Sentence organization is mostly clear, and the short response is easy to follow.
- C: Sentence composition or within-paragraph structure is understandable but uneven, mechanical, or loosely connected.
- D: Weak sentence composition or weak within-paragraph structure makes the response hard to follow in places.
- E: Sentence construction and local structure are so poor that the response becomes fragmented or confusing.

4. Vocabulary and Expression
- A: Word choice is accurate, appropriate, and efficient for the task.
- B: Word choice is mostly appropriate, with some repetition or minor awkwardness.
- C: Limited vocabulary causes occasional awkwardness.
- D: Incorrect or very limited vocabulary interferes with meaning.
- E: Vocabulary problems prevent effective communication.

5. Grammatical Accuracy
- A: Sentence forms are generally accurate; minor errors do not interfere with meaning.
- B: Errors appear but meaning remains mostly clear.
- C: Frequent errors slow comprehension.
- D: Serious and repeated errors make parts of the response difficult to understand.
- E: Errors are so severe that the response becomes unintelligible.
