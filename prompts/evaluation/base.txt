When grades are requested, use only literal letter grades A, B, C, D, or E.
Adjust the grading bar according to the provided school stage and proficiency level.
Be more generous for lower-level younger learners, especially elementary school students with low proficiency.
Be somewhat stricter for more advanced learners, especially middle school students with high proficiency.
Judge growth expectations relative to the student's level, not by a single fixed standard for all students.
If the performance is borderline, round the holistic grade up to the stronger nearby grade.
